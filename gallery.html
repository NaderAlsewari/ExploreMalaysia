Kuala Lumpur Skyline

This breath taking view of the Kuala Lumpur skyline includes the famous Petronas Twin Towers, standing tall as the symbol of Malaysia's progress and ambition

Penang Street Art

Penang is famous for its vibrant street art, which beautifully reflects the island’s multicultural heritage and creativity.

Langkawi Beaches

Langkawi is known for its stunning white sandbeaches and crystal-clear waters, making it a popular destination for tourists and nature lovers alike.

Hari raya

Eid in malaysia means hari raya but the literal meaning is Great day.what you see here is ketupat is a Javanese rice cake packed inside a diamond-shaped container of woven palm leaf pouch. Also used to symbol Hari raya.

Nasi Lemak

Nasi Lemak is Malaysia's national dish, consisting of fragrant rice cooked in coconut milk, served with sambal, boiled eggs, fried anchovies, peanuts, and cucumber. It’s a staple of Malaysian breakfast and is beloved by all.

Nasi Kukus

Nasi Kukus is a traditional Malaysian dish where rice is steamed and served with fried chicken, curry, sambal, and various sides. Its unique cooking method gives it a rich flavor and fluffy texture.

A'Famosa Fort

A'Famosa Fort in Malacca was built by the Portuguese in the 16th century. It is one of the oldest surviving European architectural remains in Southeast Asia.

Bujang Valley

Bujang Valley, located in Kedah, is an ancient archaeological site containing temple ruins from early Hindu-Buddhist civilizations dating back to the 1st century AD.

Baju Kurung and Baju Melayu

Baju Kurung (for women) and Baju Melayu (for men) are traditional Malay garments that reflect Islamic values of modesty and are worn during festivals such as Hari Raya.

Kampung Laut Mosque

Kampung Laut Mosque, located in Kelantan, is one of the oldest surviving mosques in Malaysia. It reflects traditional Malay and Islamic architecture and was built in the 18th century.
